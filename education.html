Masters of Cyber Security

My advanced studies in Cyber Security provided comprehensive knowledge and hands-on experience in key areas, including:

  • Cyber Security: Covered key topics in cybersecurity, including defensive, offensive, and preventative measures. Discussed security vulnerabilities, secure software development practices, intrusion detection and prevention systems, cryptographic techniques, applied cryptography, authentication and access control mechanisms, and comprehensive information security management strategies. Explored real-world case studies to understand the practical application of these concepts.

  • Information Visualization: Focused on raising awareness of the potential offered by information visualization techniques, developing familiarity with underlying concepts, and fostering the ability to design and apply effective visualizations. Explored various tools and methodologies for visual data representation, emphasizing the importance of clarity, accuracy, and aesthetics in conveying complex information across different contexts and audiences.

  • Legal Aspects of Cyber Security: Provided an advanced examination of critical legal issues in cybersecurity. Students completed a supervised research project of up to 12,500 words. My project focused on "Illicit Activities Primarily Relating to Cybercrime on the Dark Web," analyzing the legal challenges, enforcement strategies, and the evolving landscape of cybercrime legislation. Examined case law, regulatory frameworks, and international cooperation in combating cyber threats.

  • Cryptography: Offered a comprehensive understanding of cryptography by exploring its fundamental principles and various cryptographic algorithms. Discussed the applications of these algorithms in securing communications, covered the mathematical foundations of cryptography, and addressed key management practices. Included practical exercises in designing and implementing cryptographic solutions in real-world scenarios.

  • Security for Advanced Networks: Addressed the evolving nature of networks and the corresponding security concerns brought about by new technologies and changes in the attack surface. Covered novel approaches required to effectively protect advanced networks, including modern security protocols, network segmentation, zero-trust architecture, and the integration of AI-driven security solutions. Highlighted the importance of continuous monitoring and proactive threat management.

  • Malware Analysis and Penetration Testing: Examined two critical aspects of cybersecurity: defensive techniques in malware analysis and offensive techniques in penetration testing. Covered methods for analyzing and mitigating malware threats, including static and dynamic analysis, reverse engineering, and behavioral analysis. Explored strategies for ethically testing system vulnerabilities, such as vulnerability scanning, exploitation techniques, and reporting findings to improve overall security posture.

  • Secure Cloud Application Engineering: Focused on the design, development, and testing of cloud-native applications, with a special emphasis on satisfying business requirements. Explored cloud architectures, deployment models (IaaS, PaaS, SaaS), and security considerations specific to cloud environments. Included hands-on projects involving containerization, microservices, continuous integration/continuous deployment (CI/CD) pipelines, and cloud security best practices.

  • Dissertation: Investigated the use of machine learning to identify keyboard keystrokes based on sound, contributing to the understanding and mitigation of acoustic side-channel attacks. This research aimed to uncover vulnerabilities that could be exploited by malicious actors and develop robust defense mechanisms against such attacks. Analyzed acoustic signals to correlate them with specific keyboard inputs, providing insights into the feasibility of these attacks and proposing countermeasures to enhance security in environments where sensitive information is handled.

My studies also covered key frameworks and standards critical to cyber security:

  • NIST Guidelines: We explored the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) guidelines for managing and reducing cybersecurity risks, including the NIST Cybersecurity Framework, which helps organizations identify, protect, detect, respond to, and recover from cyber threats.

  • OWASP: The course included an in-depth look at the Open Web Application Security Project (OWASP), particularly the OWASP Top 10, which highlights the ten most critical web application security risks. This resource is essential for understanding and mitigating common vulnerabilities in web applications.

  • MITRE ATT&CK Framework: We examined the MITRE ATT&CK Framework, a comprehensive knowledge base that details adversary tactics and techniques. This framework aids in understanding, detecting, and defending against cyber threats by providing insights into attacker behavior.

  • ISO 27001/27002: The course also covered ISO 27001 and ISO 27002, international standards for managing sensitive information and implementing best practices for information security controls. ISO 27001 focuses on the requirements for an Information Security Management System (ISMS), while ISO 27002 provides guidelines for selecting and managing security controls based on an organization's risk environment.

This education has equipped me with a robust skill set to tackle complex security challenges in the digital landscape.
